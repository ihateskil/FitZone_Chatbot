EXHAUSTIVE GYM, METABOLISM, STRENGTH, AND PERFORMANCE FORMULAS MASTER REFERENCE

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1: ADVANCED ENERGY EXPENDITURE & METABOLISM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 MIFFLIN-ST JEOR EQUATION (Standard Best Practice)
  - Men:   BMR = (10 * Weight_kg) + (6.25 * Height_cm) - (5 * Age_years) + 5
  - Women: BMR = (10 * Weight_kg) + (6.25 * Height_cm) - (5 * Age_years) - 161

1.2 KATCH-MCARDLE EQUATION (Most accurate if Lean Body Mass is known)
  - LBM = Weight_kg * (1 - (Body_Fat_Percentage / 100))
  - BMR = 370 + (21.6 * LBM)

1.3 HARRIS-BENEDICT EQUATION (Revised by Roza and Shizgal)
  - Men:   BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 * Weight_kg) + (4.799 * Height_cm) - (5.677 * Age_years)
  - Women: BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 * Weight_kg) + (3.098 * Height_cm) - (4.330 * Age_years)

1.4 TOTAL DAILY ENERGY EXPENDITURE (TDEE)
  - TDEE = BMR * Activity_Multiplier
  - Multipliers:
    * Sedentary (desk job, no intentional exercise): x1.2
    * Lightly Active (light exercise/sports 1-3 days/week): x1.375
    * Moderately Active (moderate exercise/sports 3-5 days/week): x1.55
    * Very Active (hard exercise/sports 6-7 days/week): x1.725
    * Extremely Active (heavy daily exercise/physical labor/two-a-days): x1.9

1.5 COMPONENTS OF TDEE BREAKDOWN
  - Thermic Effect of Food (TEF): ~10% of total daily caloric intake.
    * Protein TEF: ~20-30% of consumed protein calories.
    * Carbohydrate TEF: ~5-15% of consumed carbohydrate calories.
    * Fat TEF: ~0-3% of consumed fat calories.
  - Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT): Remainder of active calories minus EAT (Exercise Activity Thermogenesis).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: BODY COMPOSITION & ANTHROPOMETRY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 BODY MASS INDEX (BMI)
  - BMI = Weight_kg / (Height_m * Height_m)
  - Classifications: Underweight (<18.5), Normal (18.5-24.9), Overweight (25-29.9), Obese (>=30)

2.2 U.S. NAVY BODY FAT FORMULA (Using Circumferences in Centimeters)
  - Men:   BF% = 495 / (1.0324 - 0.19077 * log10(Waist_cm - Neck_cm) + 0.15456 * log10(Height_cm)) - 450
  - Women: BF% = 495 / (1.29579 - 0.35004 * log10(Waist_cm + Hip_cm - Neck_cm) + 0.22100 * log10(Height_cm)) - 450

2.3 YMCA BODY FAT FORMULA (Using Waist Circumference and Body Weight)
  - Men:   BF% = ((4.15 * Waist_inches) - (0.082 * Weight_lbs) - 98.42) / Weight_lbs * 100
  - Women: BF% = ((4.15 * Waist_inches) - (0.082 * Weight_lbs) - 76.76) / Weight_lbs * 100

2.4 LEAN BODY MASS (LBM) & FAT MASS
  - Fat Mass = Weight_kg * (Body_Fat_Percentage / 100)
  - Lean Body Mass (LBM) = Weight_kg - Fat Mass

2.5 WAIST-TO-HIP RATIO (WHR)
  - WHR = Waist_Circumference / Hip_Circumference
  - Cardiovascular Risk thresholds: Men > 0.90 | Women > 0.85

2.6 IDEAL BODY WEIGHT (Devine Formula)
  - Men:   IBW = 50.0 + 2.3 * ((Height_cm / 2.54) - 60)
  - Women: IBW = 45.5 + 2.3 * ((Height_cm / 2.54) - 60)

SECTION 3: STRENGTH, POWER, AND ONE-REP MAX (1RM)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 VARIANT 1RM ESTIMATION FORMULAS (W = Weight Lifted, R = Repetitions Completed)
  - Epley:        1RM = W * (1 + (R / 30))  [Best for R <= 10]
  - Brzycki:      1RM = W / (1.0278 - (0.0278 * R)) [Highly accurate up to 10 reps]
  - Lander:       1RM = (100 * W) / (101.3 - (2.6712 * R))
  - Lombardi:     1RM = W * (R ^ 0.10)
  - O'Conner:     1RM = W * (1 + (0.025 * R))
  - Wathan:       1RM = (100 * W) / (48.8 + (53.8 * e^(-0.075 * R)))
  - Mayhew et al: 1RM = (100 * W) / (52.2 + (47.8 * e^(-0.055 * R)))

3.2 REPETITION INTENSITY PERCENTAGES
  - Weight for Target Reps = Estimated_1RM * Percentage_Intensity
  - Standard Percentages based on Brzycki:
    * 1 Rep  = 100% | 2 Reps = 95%  | 3 Reps = 93%  | 4 Reps = 90%
    * 5 Reps = 87%  | 6 Reps = 85%  | 7 Reps = 83%  | 8 Reps = 80%
    * 9 Reps = 77%  | 10 Reps = 75% | 12 Reps = 70% | 15 Reps = 65%

3.3 POWER OUTPUT ESTIMATION (Sayers Equation for Peak Power)
  - Peak Power (Watts) = (60.7 * Vertical_Jump_Height_cm) + (45.3 * Weight_kg) - 2055

3.4 POWERLIFTING COMPETITIVE COEFFICIENTS (Relative Strength Scoring)
  - DOTS Score, Wilks Score, and IPF GL Points utilize custom polynomial functions based on exact bodyweight and biological sex to compare the relative strength of athletes across completely different weight classes.
  - Relative Strength Ratio = Total_Weight_Lifted_kg / Bodyweight_kg

SECTION 4: CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONING & ENDURANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 ESTIMATED MAXIMUM HEART RATE (MHR)
  - Standard/Fox Formula: MHR = 220 - Age
  - Tanaka Formula (More accurate for older adults): MHR = 208 - (0.7 * Age)
  - Gellish Formula: MHR = 207 - (0.7 * Age)

4.2 TARGET HEART RATE (THR) VIA KARVONEN FORMULA (Heart Rate Reserve method)
  - Heart Rate Reserve (HRR) = Max_Heart_Rate - Resting_Heart_Rate
  - THR = (HRR * %Intensity) + Resting_Heart_Rate
  - Target Intensities:
    * Zone 1 (Recovery/Warm-up): 50% - 60%
    * Zone 2 (Aerobic Base / Fat Oxidation): 60% - 70%
    * Zone 3 (Tempo / Aerobic Endurance): 70% - 80%
    * Zone 4 (Anaerobic Threshold / Lactate Threshold): 80% - 90%
    * Zone 5 (VO2 Max Interval Training): 90% - 100%

4.3 ESTIMATED VO2 MAX ACCORDING TO PERFORMANCE PIPELINES
  - Cooper 12-Minute Run Test: VO2 Max = (Distance_in_meters - 504.9) / 44.73
  - Rockport 1-Mile Walk Test: 
    VO2 Max = 132.853 - (0.0769 * Weight_lbs) - (0.3877 * Age) + (6.315 * Gender) - (3.2649 * Time_minutes) - (0.1565 * Heart_Rate)
    [Where Gender: Men = 1, Women = 0]

SECTION 5: NUTRITION, HYDRATION, AND PERFORMANCE MACROS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 TARGET CALORIC TARGETS BASED ON FITNESS GOAL
  - Fat Loss Deficit Range:   TDEE - 300 to 700 kcal
  - Muscle Gain Surplus Range: TDEE + 200 to 500 kcal
  - Maintenance Range:        TDEE +/- 100 kcal

5.2 MACRONUTRIENT UNIT TARGETS
  - Daily Protein Target:
    * Sedentary / General Health: 0.8g per kg of body weight
    * Endurance Athlete: 1.2g to 1.4g per kg of body weight
    * Resistance Training / Muscle Building: 1.6g to 2.2g per kg of body weight
  - Daily Fat Target: 20% to 35% of total TDEE calories.
  - Daily Carbohydrate Target: Remaining Caloric Balance / 4 kcal per gram.

5.3 HYDRATION & SWEAT RATE FORMULAS
  - Baseline Daily Hydration: Bodyweight_kg * 0.035 liters (or roughly 35ml per kg)
  - Active Workout Sweat Rate: 
    Sweat Loss (Liters) = (Weight_Before_Workout_kg - Weight_After_Workout_kg) + Fluid_Consumed_During_Workout_Liters
    * Rehydration Target: Consume 1.5 Liters of water for every 1 kg of body weight lost during performance.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: TRAINING VOLUME & LOAD MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 VOLUME LOAD (Tonnage)
  - Volume Load = Sets * Reps * Weight_Lifted

6.2 INTENSITY RELATIVE VOLUME (IRV)
  - IRV = Sets * Reps * %1RM (written as a decimal)
  - Optimal threshold metrics state that IRV should sit between 20 and 30 per exercise for optimal hypertrophic stimulation without overtraining.

6.3 ACUTE TO CHRONIC WORKLOAD RATIO (ACWR) - Injure Prevention Metric
  - Acute Workload = Total training volume load completed in the current week (7 days).
  - Chronic Workload = Average weekly training volume load over the past 4 weeks (28 days).
  - ACWR = Acute Workload / Chronic Workload
  - Injury Danger Zone Thresholds:
    * Sweet Spot (Optimal fitness progression & low injury risk): 0.8 to 1.3
    * Danger Zone (High risk of structural fatigue or overuse injury): > 1.5
