===== SPORT-SPECIFIC TRAINING & PROGRAMMING GUIDE =====

SECTION 1: POWERLIFTING
- Three lifts: Squat, Bench Press, Deadlift. Goal: Maximize 1RM in each.
- Training frequency: Most programs train each lift 2-4x/week (varies by specialization and experience level).
- RPE scale (rate of perceived exertion): 1-10 scale. RPE 10 = maximal effort. RPE 7 = 3 reps left in tank.
- Periodization: Linear periodization (volume decreases as intensity increases over weeks), Block periodization (focused blocks), Conjugate/WSBB (rotate exercises and intensities).
- Popular programs: Starting Strength (novice), StrongLifts 5x5 (novice), 5/3/1 (intermediate), Sheiko (advanced), Smolov (advanced squat).
- Meet preparation: Peak phase begins 4-6 weeks out. Test openers 3 weeks out. Deload week before meet.
- The big three totals: Novice (2.5x BW), Intermediate (3.5x BW), Advanced (4.5x BW), Elite (5.5x BW).

SECTION 2: OLYMPIC WEIGHTLIFTING
- Two lifts: Snatch (bar from floor to overhead in one motion) and Clean & Jerk (bar from floor to shoulders, then overhead).
- Primary qualities: Power, speed, mobility, and technique under heavy load.
- Snatch: Wide grip, high pull, aggressive turnover, overhead squat receive.
- Clean & Jerk: Clean (deadlift to front rack position) + Jerk (dip-drive to split or squat receive).
- Pull variations: Hang pulls, clean/snatch pulls, from blocks, from deficits.
- Squat variations: Front squat (more quad focus, upright torso) and overhead squat (mobility-dependent).
- Assistance work: Good mornings, RDLs, presses, rows, pulls-ups.
- Programming: Frequency 4-6x/week. Technique work first (lighter loads), then strength work, then accessories.
- Competition: 3 attempts per lift. Heaviest successful lift counts. Total = Snatch + Clean & Jerk.

SECTION 3: BODYBUILDING
- Goal: Muscle hypertrophy (size) and aesthetics. Symmetry, proportion, and conditioning.
- Rep ranges: 8-15 reps for hypertrophy (mechanical tension + metabolic stress). Heavy (1-5) for strength, light (15-30) for pump and blood flow.
- Training splits: Push/Pull/Legs (PPL), upper/lower, body part split (bro split), full body.
- Progression: Progressive overload (adding weight, reps, sets, or reducing rest). Double progression: add reps first, then weight.
- Periodization: Accumulation (high volume), intensification (high intensity), realization (peaking).
- Competition prep: 12-16 weeks. Calorie deficit, increased cardio, maintained protein intake. Peak week: water and carb manipulation.
- Posing: Practice mandatory poses (front double biceps, back lat spread, etc.) weekly.

SECTION 4: CROSSFIT
- Constantly varied, functional movements, performed at high intensity.
- Key domains: Cardiovascular/respiratory endurance, stamina, strength, flexibility, power, speed, coordination, agility, balance, accuracy.
- WOD types: For time (complete work as fast as possible), AMRAP (as many rounds as possible in set time), EMOM (every minute on the minute).
- Benchmark WODs: Fran (21-15-9 thrusters and pull-ups), Cindy (5 pull-ups, 10 push-ups, 15 squats in 20 min AMRAP), Murph (1 mile run, 100 pull-ups, 200 push-ups, 300 squats, 1 mile run).
- Common movements: Box jumps, wall balls, kettlebell swings, burpees, double-unders, rowing, assault bike.
- Scaling: RX (prescribed weights/movements), scaled (modified weights/movements), Rx+ (harder version).
- Risk management: High injury rate from competitive intensity. Scale aggressively on form, not ego.

SECTION 5: STRONGMAN
- Events: Deadlift (regular, axle, frame, car), overhead press (log, axle, circus dumbbell), carrying (farmer's walk, yoke walk, sandbag), pulling (truck pull, sled), throwing (stone loading, Atlas stones).
- Grip strength is the primary limiting factor. Dedicated grip work essential (fat bar, pinch, wrist work).
- Energy systems: Events are typically 30-90 seconds of maximal effort, repeated with rest.
- Core strength: Anti-rotation, anti-flexion, and isometric holds are crucial.
- Training approach: Practice events regularly. Supplement with powerlifting and bodybuilding work.

SECTION 6: MARTIAL ARTS & COMBAT SPORTS
- Strength for combat: Explosive power, grip strength, neck strength, core stability.
- Endurance: Sport-specific intervals (e.g., 3-min rounds with 1-min rest). High heart rate tolerance.
- Weight cutting: 1-2 weeks before weigh-in. Water manipulation (increase then cut water, sodium/carb manipulation). Dangerous if done improperly.
- Boxing: Rotational power, footwork, shoulder endurance. Heavy bag, speed bag, double-end bag.
- BJJ/Judo: Grip endurance, isometric strength, flexibility. Gi vs no-gi grip demands differ.

SECTION 7: SPORTS-SPECIFIC CONDITIONING
- Sprint sports (track, soccer, basketball): Repeat sprint ability (RSA). Short bursts of 5-30 seconds with incomplete recovery.
- Field sports (rugby, football, hockey): Mix of aerobic base and repeated high-intensity efforts. Multidirectional movement and change of direction.
- Endurance sports (marathon, triathlon): Aerobic base built over months. Periodization of volume and intensity. Sport-specific form work.
- Racquet sports (tennis, squash): Multi-directional and reactionary. Lateral movement, change of direction, overhead power.
- Skill vs strength: Early off-season: strength focus. Late off-season: power and speed. In-season: maintenance.

SECTION 8: AGE-GROUP & MASTERS ATHLETES (35+)
- Recovery is slower. Need more recovery days, lower volume, same or higher intensity.
- Hormonal changes: Testosterone decreases, impacting muscle protein synthesis and recovery.
- Periodization becomes more important. Deload every 3-4 weeks instead of 4-6.
- Joint health: Prioritize mobility work. Avoid high-impact movements in older athletes.
- Injury prevention: Longer warm-ups (15-20 min). More prehab work for vulnerable areas.
- Strength maintenance: Lighter loads but maintain intensity relative to 1RM.

SECTION 9: COMPETITION PREP & TAPERING
- Taper: Systematic reduction of training load before competition to maximize performance.
- Duration: Typically 7-14 days. Volume reduced by 40-60%. Intensity maintained or slightly reduced.
- Peaking: 5-10% improvement in performance expected from proper taper.
- Deload vs taper: Deload is recovery from training stress. Taper is active recovery with performance timing.
- Nutrition before competition: Carb loading for endurance events (2-3 days). Normal intake for strength events. Hydration 48 hours out.
- Competition day routine: Wake time, pre-competition meal (2-3 hours before), warm-up protocol, mental preparation.

SECTION 10: SOCCER / FOOTBALL
- Demands: Mix of aerobic endurance (10-12 km per game) and high-intensity sprints (800-1200 m of sprinting per game). Change of direction every 2-4 seconds.
- Strength priorities: Lower body power (squat, trap bar deadlift, single-leg work), hamstring injury prevention (Nordic curls, Romanian deadlifts), core stability for change of direction.
- Conditioning: HIIT with multidirectional movements, repeated sprint ability training, small-sided games for sport-specific conditioning.
- Periodization: Off-season (strength focus, 3-4x/week lifting + conditioning), Pre-season (power and sport-specific conditioning, 2-3x/week lifting + increased conditioning), In-season (maintenance, 1-2x/week lifting + tactical conditioning).
- Injury prevention: FIFA 11+ warm-up program (20 min, 2-3x/week) reduces injury rates by 30-50%. Hamstring and groin strengthening, landing mechanics, and balance training are critical.

SECTION 11: BASKETBALL
- Demands: Vertical jumping (50-70 jumps per game), multidirectional sprinting (4-6 km total), quick changes of direction, upper body for passing and shooting.
- Strength priorities: Lower body power (squat, deadlift, Olympic lifts or variations), single-leg strength (lunges, split squats, step-ups), calf and ankle strength for jumping.
- Conditioning: Intervals mimicking game play (30-90 sec high intensity, 1-2 min rest), repeated jump training, change of direction drills.
- Vertical jump training: Squat strength (1.5-2x bodyweight for advanced), plyometrics (box jumps, depth jumps, bounding), Olympic lift variations (hang clean, power clean), amortization phase reduction (quick transitions from eccentric to concentric).
- Injury prevention: Ankle strengthening (single-leg balance, wobble board), ACL prevention (landing mechanics, hamstring strengthening, plyometric progression), patellar tendon management (load management, isometrics).

SECTION 12: AMERICAN FOOTBALL
- Demands: Explosive power for short bursts (5-10 seconds max effort), strength for blocking and tackling, speed and agility for position-specific demands.
- Position-specific training: Linemen (absolute strength, power clean, bench press, squat 2+ x BW), Skill positions (speed, agility, change of direction, vertical jump), Linebackers and tight ends (hybrid strength and speed).
- Strength priorities: Full body power (squat, deadlift, bench press, Olympic lifts), neck strengthening (for impact protection), grip strength, hip hinge power.
- Conditioning: Intervals matching play duration (5-10 sec max effort, 30-60 sec rest), position-specific drills, agility and change of direction.
- Periodization: Off-season (strength and hypertrophy, 4x/week), Pre-season (power and sport-specific, 3x/week + practice), In-season (maintenance lifting 2x/week + games/practice), Post-season (active recovery 2-4 weeks).

SECTION 13: SWIMMING
- Demands: Upper body endurance (lat, shoulder, core strength), lower body kick power, high aerobic + anaerobic capacity. Events range from 50m sprints (20-30 sec) to 1500m (15-20 min).
- Strength priorities: Lat pull-down and pull-up strength (for pull phase), shoulder health (rotator cuff, scapular stability), core for body position and rotation, leg power for kick.
- Dry land training: 3-4x/week off-season, 2x/week in-season. Pull-ups, rows, lat pull-downs, core work, medicine ball throws, jump training.
- Periodization: Off-season (general strength, hypertrophy focus, 4x/week), Pre-season (power and endurance, 3x/week + increased pool volume), In-season (maintenance 2x/week), Taper (reduced volume, maintained intensity).
- Shoulder health: Swimmer's shoulder is the most common injury (70% of swimmers experience shoulder pain). Prevention: external rotation strengthening, scapular retraction, posterior cuff work, lat stretching, avoiding overuse.

SECTION 14: CYCLING
- Demands: Lower body muscular endurance, high aerobic capacity (VO2 max 70-85 ml/kg/min for elite), sustained power output for 1-6 hours.
- Strength priorities: Lower body strength (squat, leg press, Bulgarian split squat), core stability for aero position maintenance, hip and knee health.
- Gym training: Off-season (heavy lower body 2x/week + accessories), In-season (maintenance 1x/week, lighter loads, higher reps, focus on muscular endurance).
- Periodization: Base (long rides at low intensity, 80% of training), Build (threshold intervals, VO2 max work, 4-8 weeks), Peak (race-specific intensity, reduced volume), Race/Taper (low volume, high intensity spikes).
- Common issues: Patellofemoral pain (from bike fit issues, quad/hamstring imbalance), Lower back pain (from prolonged aero position, weak core), Neck pain (from head position), Ulnar nerve compression (from handlebar pressure).

SECTION 15: BASEBALL / SOFTBALL
- Demands: Explosive rotational power for hitting and throwing, sprint speed (short bursts), upper body strength for throwing velocity, agility for fielding.
- Strength priorities: Rotational power (medicine ball throws, cable rotations, landmine presses), lower body power (squat, deadlift, hip hinge variations), shoulder health (rotator cuff, scapular stability, sleeper stretch).
- Throwing velocity: Linked to lower body power (hip and core rotation generates 50%+ of throwing force). Training: med ball rotational throws, hip mobility, resistance band rotational work.
- Periodization: Off-season (strength and power, 3-4x/week), Pre-season (power and sport-specific, 3x/week + practice), In-season (maintenance 2x/week, minimize fatigue), Post-season (active recovery, address injuries).
- Injury prevention: UCL (ulnar collateral ligament) protection from Tommy John surgery risk. Manage pitch counts, strengthen forearm flexors, proper throwing mechanics, avoid year-round throwing.
