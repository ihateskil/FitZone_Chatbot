===== SUPPLEMENTS & DIETARY AIDES GUIDE =====

SECTION 1: PROTEIN SUPPLEMENTS
- Whey protein: Fast-digesting milk protein. Ideal post-workout (20-40g). Contains all EAAs and BCAAs. Types: concentrate (70-80% protein), isolate (90%+), hydrolysate (pre-digested for fastest absorption).
- Casein protein: Slow-digesting milk protein (forms gel in stomach, releases amino acids over 6-8 hours). Ideal before bed or between meals.
- Plant proteins: Pea, rice, hemp, soy, pumpkin seed. Usually lower in one or more EAAs; combine sources (e.g., pea + rice) for complete profile.
- Egg white protein: Fat-free, lactose-free, moderate digestion speed.
- Collagen peptides: Not a complete protein (low tryptophan). Benefits for skin, joints, tendons, and bone health. Take 10-15g/day.

SECTION 2: AMINO ACIDS
- BCAAs (Leucine, Isoleucine, Valine): Leucine is the primary mTOR activator (muscle protein synthesis trigger). BCAAs are less effective when total protein intake is adequate. Dose: 5-10g.
- EAA/EAAs (Essential Amino Acids): 9 EAAs your body cannot produce. More effective than BCAAs alone. Dose: 6-12g.
- Beta-alanine: Increases intramuscular carnosine levels, buffers hydrogen ions (reduces fatigue during high-rep sets). Causes harmless tingling (paresthesia). Dose: 3-6g/day, split. Takes 2-4 weeks to saturate.
- Citrulline malate: Increases nitric oxide, improves blood flow, reduces fatigue. Dose: 6-8g pre-workout.
- L-glutamine: Conditionally essential during stress. Supports immune function and gut health. Limited evidence for muscle growth. Dose: 5-10g/day.
- Taurine: Supports hydration, electrolyte balance, and muscle contractility. Found in energy drinks (but those also contain caffeine).
- Creatine: SEE SECTION 4.

SECTION 3: PRE-WORKOUT INGREDIENTS
- Caffeine: Adenosine receptor antagonist. Improves focus, energy, and performance. Dose: 100-400mg pre-workout. Tolerance builds; cycle 2 weeks off every 8-12 weeks.
- Beta-alanine: (see Section 2) Tingling sensation harmless.
- Citrulline malate: (see Section 2) Improves pump and endurance.
- Nitrates (beetroot extract): Convert to nitric oxide. Improves blood flow and endurance. Dose: 400-500mg (as extract) or 500ml beetroot juice.
- Betaine (trimethylglycine): May improve power output and hydration. Dose: 2.5-5g/day.
- Tyrosine: Dopamine precursor. Helps with focus during prolonged sessions. Dose: 500-2000mg.
- Agmatine sulfate: Nitric oxide booster with potential mood effects. Dose: 500-1500mg.

SECTION 4: CREATINE
- Creatine monohydrate: Most researched, most effective, cheapest form. Dose: 5g/day (no loading needed). 20g/day for 5-7 days for faster saturation (loading phase).
- Other forms: Creatine HCl, ethyl ester, nitrate, etc. No evidence they are superior to monohydrate.
- Benefits: Increased strength (5-15%), power output, muscle mass (via increased training volume), cognitive function, bone density.
- Timing: Any time of day, consistent daily intake matters more than timing. Post-workout may be slightly better.
- No loading needed: 5g/day reaches saturation in ~3-4 weeks. Loading (20g/day for 7 days) saturates in 5-7 days.
- Safety: Well-studied, safe for long-term use (studies up to 5+ years). Not harmful to kidneys in healthy individuals. Causes intracellular water retention (not subcutaneous bloat).
- Vegetarians/vegans see greater benefits (lower baseline creatine stores).

SECTION 5: CAFFEINE & STIMULANTS
- Caffeine mechanism: Blocks adenosine receptors (reduces perceived effort, increases alertness).
- Effective dose: 3-6 mg/kg bodyweight (210-420mg for 70kg person) taken 30-60 min pre-workout.
- Half-life: 3-6 hours. Avoid afternoon intake to prevent sleep disruption.
- Tolerance: Regular use reduces effects. 2-week breaks reset sensitivity.
- Stacking: Often combined with L-theanine (200mg) to reduce jitters and improve focus.
- Sources: Coffee, tea, pre-workout supplements, caffeine anhydrous, energy drinks.
- Yohimbine: Alpha-2 antagonist, may aid fat loss when taken fasted. Risk of anxiety and elevated heart rate. Dose: 0.2 mg/kg.

SECTION 6: WEIGHT LOSS SUPPLEMENTS
- Caffeine: Mild thermogenic and appetite suppressant.
- Green tea extract (EGCG): Mild thermogenic effect. Catechins + caffeine synergistic. Dose: 400-500mg EGCG/day.
- Cayenne/capsaicin: Increases thermogenesis and fat oxidation. Dose: 2-4mg capsaicin per meal.
- Glucomannan: Soluble fiber that expands in stomach, increasing satiety. Dose: 1g before meals with water.
- CLA (conjugated linoleic acid): Very modest fat loss (~0.1kg/month). Not worth the cost for most.
- L-carnitine: May improve fat oxidation during exercise. Oral bioavailability is poor (14-18%). Dose: 500-2000mg.
- Raspberry ketones, Garcinia cambogia, and most fat burners: Weak to no evidence. Avoid.
- GLP-1 agonists (Ozempic, Mounjaro): Prescription medications for diabetes/obesity. Not supplements. Significant side effects.

SECTION 7: VITAMINS & MINERALS
- Vitamin D: Crucial for bone health, immune function, and testosterone. Most people are deficient. Dose: 1000-4000 IU/day. Take with fat for absorption.
- Magnesium: Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions (including muscle contraction, protein synthesis, sleep). Dose: 300-400mg/day. Forms: glycinate (sleep), citrate (digestion), malate (energy), threonate (cognitive).
- Zinc: Supports testosterone production, immune function, and recovery. Dose: 15-30mg/day. Take with food. High doses (50mg+ daily) can cause copper deficiency.
- Vitamin B12: Energy metabolism, red blood cell formation. Vegans at risk of deficiency. Dose: 2.4mcg/day (oral).
- Iron: Oxygen transport. Women and athletes at higher risk of deficiency. Do NOT supplement unless deficient (excess iron is toxic). Dose: 8-18mg/day if needed.
- Omega-3 (EPA/DHA, fish oil): Anti-inflammatory, supports heart and brain health. Dose: 1-3g/day EPA+DHA. Ratio EPA:DHA of 2:1 is common.
- Vitamin C: Antioxidant, collagen synthesis, immune function. Dose: 100-200mg/day (higher for colds).
- Vitamin K2: Directs calcium to bones, away from arteries. Works with Vitamin D. Dose: 90-180mcg K2 MK-7.
- Calcium: Bone health, muscle contraction. Most get enough from diet. Dose: 1000-1200mg/day total (diet + supplements).
- Ashwagandha: Adaptogenic herb. May reduce cortisol and improve testosterone (modest effects). Dose: 300-600mg/day with standardized withanolides.

SECTION 8: JOINT HEALTH & RECOVERY
- Omega-3 (see Section 7): Anti-inflammatory for joints.
- Glucosamine sulfate: May slow cartilage breakdown in osteoarthritis. Dose: 1500mg/day. Takes 4-8 weeks to show effect.
- Chondroitin: Often paired with glucosamine. Supports cartilage structure. Dose: 800-1200mg/day.
- MSM (methylsulfonylmethane): May reduce joint pain and inflammation. Dose: 1-3g/day.
- Collagen peptides: May improve joint health and reduce activity-related joint pain. Dose: 10-15g/day.
- Turmeric/curcumin: Anti-inflammatory. Poor bioavailability; pair with black pepper (piperine) for 2000% increase in absorption. Dose: 500-1000mg curcuminoids/day.
- Boswellia serrata: Anti-inflammatory for joint health. Dose: 300-500mg 2-3x/day.

SECTION 9: SLEEP & RECOVERY SUPPLEMENTS
- Melatonin: Regulates circadian rhythm. Effective for jet lag and sleep onset. Dose: 0.5-5mg 30-60 min before bed. Lower doses (0.5-1mg) may be as effective as higher.
- Magnesium glycinate: Promotes relaxation and sleep quality. Dose: 200-400mg before bed.
- L-theanine: Increases alpha brain waves (relaxation without sedation). Dose: 100-400mg. Often stacked with caffeine (reduces jitters) or taken before bed.
- 5-HTP: Serotonin precursor. May improve sleep and mood. Dose: 50-200mg before bed. Do NOT combine with SSRIs (serotonin syndrome risk).
- Apigenin: Calming compound from chamomile. Binds to GABA receptors. Dose: 50mg.
- Glycine: Can improve sleep quality and reduce sleep onset time. Dose: 3g before bed.
- GABA: Direct GABA supplementation has limited brain penetration. May still help with relaxation via gut-brain axis. Dose: 250-750mg.
- ZMA (Zinc Magnesium Aspartate): Popular sleep/recovery formula. Provides zinc, magnesium, and vitamin B6.

SECTION 10: SUPPLEMENT SAFETY & STACKING
- Third-party testing: Look for NSF Certified for Sport, Informed Sport, or USP verification. These verify content accuracy and absence of banned substances.
- Supplements are not regulated like drugs by the FDA. Manufacturer is responsible for safety.
- Common contaminant risks: Heavy metals (lead, arsenic), fillers, undeclared stimulants, anabolic steroids (in prohormone products).
- Avoid proprietary blends (hide exact dosages).
- Cycle recommendations: Stimulants (2 weeks on, 2 weeks off or as needed). Most others (creatine, protein, vitamins) are fine continuously.
- Medication interactions: St. John's Wort (reduces birth control/antidepressant effectiveness), Vitamin K (interferes with blood thinners), iron (reduces thyroid med absorption).
- Pregnant/nursing: Consult doctor before any supplement. Avoid high-dose caffeine, yohimbine, and herbal blends.
- Stack compatibility: Caffeine + L-theanine (good synergy). Avoid multiple stimulants simultaneously. Separate calcium and iron from thyroid medication by 4 hours.
